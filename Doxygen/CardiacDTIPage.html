Cardiac Diffusion Tensor Imaging

Introduction

This package is meant to deal with the processing of Diffusion Tensor Images (DTI) , when they are applied to cardiac tissues.

DTI is an MR modality that measures water diffusion in living tissue. By measuring specific diffusion in several directions over the sphere, we are able to reconstruct the spatial covariant matrix of the global diffusion process. As this matrix has 6 degrees of freedom, we need 6 non-colinear directional measures of the diffusion. The covariant matrix is also called tensor , and consists in a symmetrix positive definite matrix. The set of symmetrix positive definite matrices does not form a vector space, and thus making difficult processing them (average, statistics, etc).

Coordinates

The cardiac muscle, and especially the Left Ventricle (LV) has a very characteristic shape that is usually represented as a truncated ellipsoid . By taking this property into consideration, this package uses adapted curvilinear coordinates, also called Prolate Spheroid Coordinates (PPS) in order to adapt any process to the cardiac LV shape.

To Compile

Most of the tools present in this package will need ITK and VTK to be compiled properly. The tools related to tensors will need the TensorToolKit [yet to be released]. Sorry for this high external lib dependency.

You also need to have CMake installed in your computer in order to generate a makefile specific for your OS.

After installing external toolkits and generate the project, just hit make in your command prompt, or compile all targets from your favourite code editor.
